Julia Child's Coq au Vin

Ingredients

  • 4 chicken thighs
  • 4 chicken Drumsticks
  • 1 1/2 cups of red wine
  • 1 cup chicken stock
  • 1/4 cup of brandy
  • 3 strips of lardons
  • 1 teaspoon sea salt and pepper
  • 1 medium onion
  • 4 medium carrots
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons thyme leaves
  • 8 ounces of mushrooms
  • 8 ounces of pearl onions
  • Beurre Manie
  1. Place the chicken thighs and drumsticks in a medium-sized bowl and pour the wine, chicken stock, and (if using) the brandy over the top. Prep the vegetables.
  2. Add the lardon to a large, high-sided pan or braiser over medium-high heat. Cook until the lardon is crispy, about 8 minutes, then remove it from the pan with a slotted spoon.
  3. Remove the chicken from the wine marinade and dry the chicken well with paper towels. Season the chicken with ½ teaspoon of salt and pepper.
  4. Working in 2 batches if needed, place the chicken in the pan, skin side down. Sear until golden on both sides (about 8-10 minutes total), then remove the chicken. Remove all but two tablespoons of the lardon/chicken oil from the pan – reserving the oil to use later in the recipe.
  5. Add the sliced onion and carrots to the pan and let them cook until the onion is golden brown, about 7-8 minutes. Add the garlic to the pan and let it cook for 1 minute.
  6. Push the vegetables to the side of the pan and add the tomato paste. Cook the tomato paste until it is fragrant and begins to darken. Pour in the reserved wine marinade, add the remaining ½ teaspoon of salt and pepper, and bring it to a boil for 5 minutes, scraping the bottom to remove any stuck-on bits.
  7. Nestle the chicken into the pan and sprinkle the thyme over top. Cover the pan, turn the heat to low, and simmer for 20 minutes.
  8. Pour 1 tablespoon of the reserved oil (or use olive oil) into a large skillet. Add the mushrooms and saute over medium-high heat until brown, about 10 minutes.
  9. Add the pearl onions to the pan with the chicken and cook for 10 minutes.
  10. In a small bowl, mix your choice of beurre manie. Remove the chicken from the pan, add the beurre manie, and stir until the sauce thickens. Season to taste with salt and pepper
  11. Add the chicken back into the pan and top with the cooked bacon and mushrooms. Sprinkle with a fresh thyme.
